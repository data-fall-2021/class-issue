Golden Globes Are Still Mostly White

Big Surprise

By Sarah Kerson, Amanda Kari McHugh and Monique Mulima

After a year marred by various accounts of scandal and controversy, this year’s Golden Globe nominees show little improvement in racial diversity. Three-quarters of this year’s nominees are white. It displays small progress for an organization that has historically excluded non-white performers. In 2019, only two people of color won film or television Golden Globes: Awkwafina for “The Farewell” and Ramy Youssef for “Ramy.”

In May, NBC announced it would not air the awards ceremony after an investigation by the Los Angeles Times brought scrutiny to the Hollywood Foreign Press Association, the body that awards the Golden Globes. Among allegations facing the HFPA are its lack of diversity in its voting body and accepting bribes in exchange for nominations. It remains unclear where the ceremony will be broadcast.
